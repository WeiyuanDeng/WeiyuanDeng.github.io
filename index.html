About Me STRONG INTEREST, STRONG WILL

The seed of researching into the medical robotics and imaging was rooted in my mind during my graduate study at Northwestern University. From my working experiences as an algorithm engineer, I confirmed that I have a strong desire to seek advanced study in graduate school in order to conduct research of interest in medical robotics and go further in the specific field in the future, so that my knowledge and past experience in both engineering and computer science could be applied to the area and thus develop the expertise to better serve my future further study at PhD level.

During my study, BSc Theoretical and Applied Mechanics programme in China has laid stress on the theoretical study in science and mathematics, while MSc Robotics in the US and MSc CGVI in the UK have improved my integrated abilities in programming tremendously through research projects. During my study in different cultures, I learned a lot of knowledge from different disciplinary and quickly applied them into research and projects.

I would like to contribute my ideas and passion in the field, and hopefully, become an outstanding female researcher. I am eager to bring my passion, skills, and experiences to the vibrant academic community.

Procedural Generation

                                

                                    

                                

           
                                
Scroll down to view more
                                

                                
                                
                                
                                
This project aims to procedurally generate the a flock of fish and posidonia oceanica, the
                                    underwater vegetation, at the ancient underwater archaeological site of Amathus in Cyprus.
                                    The project is implemented in Unity and is mostly used for virtual reality simulations for
                                    people who are not able to dive to the scene.

                                
With this project, a virtual reality environment is presented. The picture opens on an
                                    underwater landscape with posidonia oceanica and rocks nearby. The main objective of
                                    this project is to provide users with the opportunity to fully explore the underwater environment while also providing them with the opportunity to learn about archaeology by
                                    walking on the sea floor and interacting with ancient artefacts. This could provide users
                                    with an immersive experience without compromising the preservation of the historic sites.
